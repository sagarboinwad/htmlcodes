Idli Recipe


Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 cup of urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as required for batter consistency)

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice and urad dal separately. Soak them in water for about 4-6 hours.
  2. Soak the fenugreek seeds along with the urad dal for better fermentation.
  3. After soaking, grind the rice and dal separately into a smooth batter using a wet grinder or blender. Add water as needed to get a thick but pourable consistency.
  4. Mix the rice and dal batters together in a large bowl. Add salt and mix well.
  5. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for about 8-12 hours, depending on the weather. The batter should double in size and become slightly bubbly.
  6. Once the batter is fermented, grease the idli molds with a little oil or ghee.
  7. Pour the batter into the idli molds and steam the idlis in a steamer or pressure cooker (without the weight) for 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean when inserted.
  8. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Note: You can adjust the consistency of the batter by adding more or less water. The key to soft idlis is proper fermentation, so ensure your batter is left to ferment in a warm environment.
